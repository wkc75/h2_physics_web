# **Rotational Equilibrium and Translational Equilibrium**

## **Rotational Equilibrium and Principle of Moment**

<InlineCitation
  label="2g"
  content="apply the principle of moments to new situations or to solve related problems."
/>

<Callout type="definition" title="Condition for Rotational Equilibrium">
  The net moment on the body **about any point** is zero
</Callout>

<Callout type="definition" title="Principle of Moment">
  **Principle of moments** states that for a body to be in Rotational
  equilibrium, the sum of clockwise moments about any point equals the sum of
  anticlockwise moment about the same point.
</Callout>

## **Translational Equilibrium**

<Callout type="definition" title="Condition for Translational Equilibrium">
  The **net force** acting on the body is zero
</Callout>

For systems with 3 non-parallel forces in translational equilibrium, the 3 forces will

<div className="ml-6">

$-$ Intersect at one point

$-$ Form a vector triangle

</div>

## **Static Equilibrium**

<InlineCitation
  label="2h"
  content="show an understanding that, when there is no resultant force and no resultant torque,
            a system is in equilibrium."
/>

<Callout type="definition" title="Conditions for Static Equilibrium">
  <ul className="list-disc ml-6 space-y-1">
    <li>The net external force acting on the body is zero.</li>
    <li>The net moment on the body about any point is zero.</li>
  </ul>
</Callout>

<br />

<Callout type="exam" title="Solving Static Equilibrium Problems">

  <ol className="list-decimal ml-6 space-y-1">
    <li>Isolate the body of interest.</li>

    <li>
      Draw the free-body diagram and identify all forces acting on the body.
    </li>

    <li>
      Set up the torque equation (condition for rotational equilibrium).
      <ul className="list-disc ml-6">
        <li>Choose a &ldquo;good&rdquo; reference point.</li>
        <li>
          A &ldquo;good&rdquo; reference point lies on the lines of action of one
          or more unknown forces, reducing the number of unknown terms.
        </li>
        <li>
          Apply the condition $ \sum \tau = 0 $,
          <strong>sum of clockwise moments = sum of anti-clockwise moments</strong>.
        </li>
      </ul>
    </li>

    <li>
      Set up the two force equations (x and y components)
      (conditions for translational equilibrium).
      <ul className="list-disc ml-6">
        <li>Choose two perpendicular directions x and y.</li>
        <li>Resolve all forces into x and y components.</li>
        <li>
          Apply $ \sum F_x = 0 $,<br />
          <strong>sum of leftward forces = sum of rightward forces</strong>.
        </li>
        <li>
          Apply $ \sum F_y = 0 $,<br />
          <strong>sum of upward forces = sum of downward forces</strong>.
        </li>
      </ul>
    </li>

    <li>Solve the equations to find the unknown forces.</li>

  </ol>
</Callout>
